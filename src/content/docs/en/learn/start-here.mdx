Escuela IA doesn't have a single entry point, because not everyone starts from the same place. This page sends you straight to the part that's useful for you, without making you read what you already know.

## Where do I start?

**I barely know anything about AI, and I don't need to know how to code.**
→ Go to [Level 0 · AI for everyone](/en/learn/level-0/). It explains what AI is in plain words, assuming nothing.

**I already use ChatGPT or similar tools, but I want to understand what I'm doing better.**
→ Go to [Level 1 · User](/en/learn/level-1/). Covers how to get more out of tools you already use, and starts introducing technical terms at a comfortable pace.

**I want to build something myself — an automation, my own assistant, running a model on my computer.**
→ Go to the [Easy AI core](/en/), where you describe what you want and it builds a concrete path for your case. Level 2 · Builder, with more build-focused detail, is in preparation (v2).

**I'm already technical and want the detail** (architectures, APIs, serious self-hosting).
→ Level 3 · Technical, the densest one, meant for people who already know how to code, is in preparation (v2) — in the meantime, the [Concepts](/en/learn/concepts/) cover several of those topics in depth.

**I ran into a term I don't understand** (API, token, GPU, Docker...).
→ [Cross-cutting concepts](/en/learn/concepts/) are short, independent pages meant to be checked once and then get back to what you were reading.

**I work in a specific field and want to know what applies to my job.**
→ [By industry](/en/by-industry/) has one page per profession: what AI can do there, real cases, tools and risks.

## How the content is organized

The levels (0 to 3) are progressive, but you don't need to read them in order or in full: each one assumes the previous one, so if something sounds unfamiliar in Level 1, it's probably explained in Level 0. Cross-cutting concepts and industry pages are independent — jump straight to whichever one you need.

The whole site is bilingual (Spanish/English) and free, with no paid affiliates. If something feels missing or wrong, the project is open — see [how to contribute](https://github.com/arnau-cell/escuela-ia/blob/master/CONTRIBUTING.md).
