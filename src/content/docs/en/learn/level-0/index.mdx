This level assumes you know nothing. If you've never used ChatGPT, if "artificial intelligence" sounds like science fiction to you, or if you just want a clear picture before moving on, start here.

## What is artificial intelligence, really?

When we talk about "AI" today, we almost always mean programs that have learned to do a task by analyzing huge numbers of examples, instead of someone programming rule after rule. An example makes it clear: nobody sits down and hand-writes the rules for "what a grammatically correct Spanish sentence sounds like" — instead, a model reads millions of real sentences and learns the pattern on its own.

That doesn't mean it "thinks" like a person. It means it's very good at **recognizing and repeating patterns** it has seen before, applied to new situations.

## The type of AI you'll deal with the most: chatbots

Tools like ChatGPT, Claude or Gemini are **language models**: programs trained on enormous amounts of text, which learned to predict which word makes the most sense after another. With that single trick — predicting the next word, over and over — they manage to hold a conversation, write a text, summarize a document, or explain a concept.

They don't "know" things the way a person does: they don't look things up in a database of verified facts, they generate the response that seems most likely given what they learned. That's why they sometimes get things confidently wrong — this is called a **hallucination**. It's not that they lie on purpose: they generate something plausible that turns out to be false. Always double-check important data (dates, figures, names) before trusting it.

## What is this actually useful for?

Some common uses that require no technical knowledge at all:

- **Writing and editing text**: emails, summaries, translations, changing the tone of something you already wrote.
- **Explaining things to you**: ask it anything as if it were an extremely patient person (and verify what matters).
- **Organizing information**: paste in a long text and ask for a summary, a list of key points, or a restructure.
- **Generating images**: describe what you want to see and a different type of model (not the text one) draws it.
- **Coding** (if you're interested): even without knowing how to code, you can ask it to explain or fix code, step by step.

## One step further: agents

So far we've talked about tools you ask something of and they respond. There's a newer type of AI, the <ConceptLink slug="que-es-un-agente-de-ia">AI agent</ConceptLink>, that can also *do* things for you (search for information, run tasks) instead of just responding. You'll see it mentioned more and more — you don't need to fully understand it yet, just know that distinction exists.

## What to remember from this level

1. Today's AI learns patterns from examples, it doesn't "think" like a person.
2. Chatbots predict the most likely word — they're very good at it, but can confidently make things up.
3. Always verify important data they give you.
4. Today it's used for writing, explaining, organizing, generating images and, increasingly, acting on your behalf.
