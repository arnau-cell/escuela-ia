## A token isn't a letter or a word

A **token** is the smallest chunk of text an AI model processes — usually part of a word, not the
whole word and not a single letter. As a rough reference: in English or Spanish, **100 tokens are
roughly 75 words** (a short word is usually 1 token; a long or uncommon word can split into 2 or 3).

## Why they charge per token, not per word

Cloud AI services charge based on **how many tokens they process**, separating what you write
("input tokens") from what the AI replies ("output tokens") — output is usually priced higher than
input, because generating it takes more work from the model than just reading it.

## A real example (not an estimate)

An affordable model like Claude Haiku 4.5 (verified against official documentation, July 2026):
**$1 USD/million input tokens, $5 USD/million output tokens.** A typical one-question-one-answer
exchange (about 1,500 input tokens + 1,500 output tokens) costs around **$0.009 USD** — less than a
cent. Model prices change frequently and are billed in dollars (the euro equivalent varies with the
exchange rate) — this figure is a concrete example, not a fixed rate forever.

## The key idea

If an AI tool is free for you, someone is paying for those tokens behind the scenes (through usage
limits, ads, or absorbing the cost as part of the business). Understanding this helps you read any
"free plan" or "daily message limit" more critically.
