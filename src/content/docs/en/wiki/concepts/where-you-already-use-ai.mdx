You don't need to open ChatGPT or Claude to be using artificial intelligence — you probably already
do, several times a day, built into apps you use for another reason entirely.

## Examples already on your phone

- **WhatsApp/Meta AI**: the assistant that shows up inside the chat, or the suggested replies it
  sometimes proposes when you answer a message.
- **Your phone's keyboard**: autocorrect and word suggestions have used AI models for years (simpler
  ones than a chat, but AI all the same).
- **Photos**: when your phone recognizes faces, removes objects from a picture, or improves a
  low-light photo's quality, AI is working behind the scenes.
- **Email**: suggested replies and spam filters have had AI built in for years.
- **Maps**: calculating the fastest route in real time, or predicting traffic, also uses AI.

## Why it's worth noticing

These examples aren't "advanced" or experimental cases — they're AI already built in, running in
the background, with nobody asking you to "learn how to use it." Telling them apart from a
conversational assistant like Claude or ChatGPT helps you understand that AI isn't one single
thing: it's a family of different technologies, each solving a specific problem (recognizing a face
isn't the same task as holding a conversation).
