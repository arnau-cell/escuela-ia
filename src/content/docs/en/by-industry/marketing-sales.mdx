## What AI can do here

Marketing is one of the sectors where generative AI shows up fastest: text drafts, ad variations, market research summaries, FAQ answers. What it's good at is producing *volume* quickly from a clear idea. What it's not good at on its own: knowing what your specific audience actually cares about, or keeping a recognizable brand voice without someone reviewing it.

## Real use cases

- **Content drafts**: blog posts, ad copy, short video scripts, from a brief.
- **Variations to test**: generating 10 versions of the same ad for A/B testing, instead of writing them one by one.
- **Research and summarizing**: summarizing market reports, customer feedback, or reviews to spot patterns without reading everything by hand.
- **First-tier customer support**: chatbots that answer frequently asked questions and escalate to a person when needed.
- **SEO**: identifying topics and questions people actually search for, and structuring content that answers them.

## Tools

You don't need a specialized paid tool to get started: a general assistant's free tier (see <ConceptLink slug="local-vs-nube">free cloud chat</ConceptLink> in [Set up your AI](/en/build/set-up-your-ai/)) already covers drafts, variations and summaries reasonably well. Specialized tools mostly add *workflow* (templates, editorial calendar integration, brand history), not capability a general assistant lacks.

| Tool | What it does | Cost | Open alternative |
|---|---|---|---|
| Copy.ai | Short copy (ads, CTAs, product descriptions), with sales workflows | No permanent free tier; plans from $29 USD/month, $24/month billed annually (verified Jul 2026) | A general cloud assistant (ChatGPT/Claude, free tier) with your own prompt template |
| Jasper | Long-form content with configurable brand voice | Paid, no permanent free tier | Same as above, spending time describing your brand voice in the prompt |
| Frase | SEO-oriented content research and structure | From $49 USD/month (verified Jul 2026) | Asking a general assistant to analyze the most-searched questions on a topic and propose a structure |

## Risks specific to this sector

- **Generic content**: if everyone uses the same kind of prompt, the output starts looking more and more alike across different brands. Reviewing and personalizing isn't optional — it's what sets your content apart from any competitor using the same tool.
- **Low-quality content saturation**: search engines and platforms are actively adjusting how they value mass-produced content published without editorial review — publishing without judgment can hurt more than it helps.
- **Customer data in third-party tools**: if you paste customer comments or data into an external tool to analyze them, check its privacy policy first — see <ConceptLink slug="local-vs-nube">local vs. cloud</ConceptLink>.
- **Transparency with your audience**: if you use AI for customer support or large-scale content generation, be honest about it when your audience asks — trust is lost fast and doesn't come back with another prompt.
